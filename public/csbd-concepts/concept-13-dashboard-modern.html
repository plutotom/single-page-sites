Compulsive Sexual Behavior Disorder (CSBD)

A brief psychoeducation guide

What is CSBD? A pattern of sexual urges or behaviors that are difficult to control and continue despite negative consequences.

Alternative Conceptualizations

  • Hypersexual Disorder / Sexual Desire Dysregulation
  • Out-of-Control Sexual Behavior
  • Behavioral Addiction Model
  • Obsessive-Compulsive Spectrum Model
  • Impulse Control Disorder Model
  • Moral Incongruence / PPMI

Key Diagnostic Features

  • Repeated failure to control sexual urges or behaviors
  • Sexual behavior becomes central in life
  • Multiple failed attempts to cut back
  • Continues despite harm or consequences
  • Causes distress or impairment for 6 months or more

Etiology / Why It May Develop

  • Trauma history
  • Emotional dysregulation
  • Depression or anxiety
  • Family history of addiction
  • Easy access to sexual content online

Prevalence + Course / Prognosis

  • Rates vary across studies
  • More commonly reported in men
  • Many people do not seek treatment
  • Often begins in late adolescence
  • Can become chronic without treatment
  • Therapy and support may help reduce symptoms

Cultural + Spiritual Considerations

  • Sexual norms differ across cultures
  • Moral or religious shame can increase distress
  • Distress from values conflict alone does not equal CSBD
